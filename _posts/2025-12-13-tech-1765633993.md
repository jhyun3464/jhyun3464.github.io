**1. Title**: Build Your Dream: The Open-Source Medium-Format Rangefinder (MRF2)

**2. Summary**:
*   Photographer Albert Cornelissen designed the MRF2, an affordable medium-format rangefinder camera that combines Mamiya Press lenses with modern LiDAR-powered focusing, microelectronics, and 3D-printed components.
*   The entire MRF2 project is open source, offering 3D-printable files, instructions, and software on Github, making it accessible for anyone to build their own customizable camera.
*   Inspired by the supportive DIY camera community, the MRF2 is designed for ease of assembly (minimal soldering), supports multiple film formats (35mm panoramas to 6x9), and includes advanced features like a light meter and horizon level.

**3. Key Takeaway**: Albert Cornelissen's MRF2 demonstrates how innovative DIY spirit, modern technology, and an open-source ethos can empower photographers to create advanced and personalized photographic tools.
